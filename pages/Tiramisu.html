How to make Tiramisu

Done correctly, a classic tiramisù can be transcendent. A creamy dessert of espresso-soaked ladyfingers surrounded by lightly sweetened whipped cream and a rich mascarpone, tiramisù relies heavily on the quality of its ingredients. If you don’t have a barista setup at home, pick up the espresso at a local coffee shop, or use strongly brewed coffee. As for the ladyfingers, make your own or buy them, but keep in mind that store-bought varieties can range from soft and spongy (like angel food cake) to hard and crunchy (like biscotti). Both kinds will work here, but if you're using the softer variety, stick to a light brushing of espresso, instead of a deep dip.

For this recipe you'll need:

  • Egg yolk
  • Sugar and heavy cream
  • Mascarpone cheese
  • Espresso or a very strong coffee
  • Rum or cognac
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder
  • About 24 ladyfingers
  • Bittersweet chocolate (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the cream mixing first the eggyolks and then the sugar and heavy cream
  2. Combine espresso and liquor in a separate bowl and set aside
  3. Divide the rice between 2 bowls. Top with salmon, avocado, cucumber and kimchi
  4. Dip each ladyfinger into the espresso mixture and place it in the bowl where we're going to make the dessert
  5. Spread half the mascarpone mixture onto the ladyfingers in one even layer. Repeat with remaining espresso-dipped ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture.
  6. Cover with plastic wrap and let chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours and then enjoy
